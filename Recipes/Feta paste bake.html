Feta pasta bake

Rev. 5/29/2022 LRM.

Found this looking for a "dairy-free" (i.e. no cow!) substitute for my typical cheesey meaty bake. Apparently the original recipe (as opposed to my modified version) has been going viral, and it did not disappoint!
Modified from https://www.delish.com/cooking/recipe-ideas/a35421563/baked-feta-pasta-tiktok/

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes -- 2 pt. cherry or grape tomatoes
  • Shallot -- 1 shallot, quartered
  • Garlic -- 3 cloves, smashed
  • Olive oil -- 1/2 c., divided
  • Sausage -- 1/2 pound of italian sausage links
  • Salt -- kosher salt, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes -- 1 pinch
  • Feta -- 8 oz block
  • Thyme -- 3 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • Pasta -- 10 oz dry pasta (ideally cavatappi)
  • Lemon zest -- zest of 1 lemon, optional
  • Fresh basil -- as garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  2. In a large ovenproof skillet or medium baking dish, combine tomatoes, shallot, garlic, and all but 1 tablespoon oil.
  3. Season with salt and red pepper flakes and toss to combine.
  4. Place feta into center of tomato mixture, add sausage links, and drizzle with remaining 1 tablespoon oil. Scatter thyme sprigs over tomatoes.
  5. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes, until tomatoes are bursting and feta is golden on top.
  6. Remove sausage links, cut into slices, and add back into baking dish.
  7. Meanwhile, in a large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta until al dente according to package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water before draining.
  8. To skillet with tomatoes and feta, add cooked pasta, reserved pasta water, and lemon zest (if using) and stir until combined.
  9. Garnish with basil and serve
