Nanxi's Dumpling Recipe

Rev. 10/9/2022 LRM.

Recipe learned from a friend back in ~2013 back when we had more Chinese friends at VT. Had a great time manufacturing dumplings for Chinese New Year a few times, and we eventually got a personalized tutorial in our apartment. Makes about 50 dumplings.

Ingredients

  • Flour -- 2 c. wheat flour
  • Meat -- 1 lb ground meat
  • Green onion -- 1 bunch of frech green onion, minced
  • Ginger -- 1/4 c. frech ginger, minced
  • Sichuan peppercorn -- 1 tbsp dry peppercorns (a.k.a. "Prickly ash seeds")
  • Soy sauce -- 1/4 c. medium soy sauce
  • Cabbage -- 1 whole Napa cabbage, minced
  • Cooking wine -- Chinese yellow cooking wine, 1/4 c (substitute boxed Chardonnay)
  • Chicken powder -- 1 tbsp (a.k.a. "Caldo de Pollo")
  • Sugar -- 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • Sesame oil -- 1/4 c.
  • Salt -- ~1/4 c.
  • Chinese 13 spice mix -- It comes in a little yellow box with a brown picture of a chinese guy wearing a hat. You can buy it at Oasis. Or substitute a five-spice mix. Or omit it entirely.

Instructions

Dough for wrapppers

  1. Add flour to mixing bowl, and slowly mix in warm water until the dough comes together.
  2. Knead thoroughly.
  3. Leave dough to sit covered in plastic wrap for 1 hour or until dumpling filling is ready

Dumpling filling

Note: Three things happen in parallel here: boiling pricklyash seeds, dry brining cabbage, and blending meat. Everything eventually is added to the meat bowl or discarded.

Note: For best results, make a day early, and refrigerate overnight.

  1. Add 1/3 cup of water to a small skillet, and bring to a boil.
  2. Add Prickly ash seeds, and boil for 5 minutes
  3. Add cabbage in a big bowl and cover with a thin layer of salt. This will start pulling the water out of the cabbage, and it will become slightly fragrant.
  4. Add meat to the bowl, and stir thoroughly (four chopsticks work well).
  5. Stir in cooking wine.
  6. Stir salt into cabbage, and squeeze out the water, reserving about 1 cup cabbage water. Do this periodically, and continue adding salt as necessary to get the water out.
  7. Pour off water from pricklyash seeds into the meat.
  8. Squeeze out ~1/2 cup of water from cabbage and add to meat. Add additional cabbage water if the meat gets too dry: it should have a consistency similar to peanut butter.
  9. Squeeze out cabbage very thoroughly so that it is as dry as possible.
  10. Add cabbage to meat. Add soy sauce to meat. It should look dark and smell noticeably like salt and soy sauce, and be sticky.
  11. Add garlic, ginger, green onion, spices, sugar, and sesame oil, and mix thoroughly.

Dumpling wrappers

  1. Flour hands and working surface.
  2. Roll dough into a balland divide into thirds.
  3. Roll dough into tubes with ~1 inch diameter.
  4. For each dumpling:
    1. Tear off 1-inch-long piece and roll it into a ball.
    2. Press ball flat with palm of hand.
    3. Using a rolling pin, roll outward from the middle while simultaneously rotating (the finished wrapper should be slightly thicker in the middle compared to the edges.
    4. Add filling, and fold closed.

Cooking instructions

  1. Bring pan of water to a rolling boil.
  2. Drop dumplings in to boil(no more than will fit floating on the surface of the water).
  3. Dumplings are almost cooked when they float (wait two minutes more).
  4. Remove pan from heat: if dumplings still float when the water is not boiling, then they are done

Extra notes

  • I only used about half of a big Napa cabage from oasis. Next time double recipe except for cabbage?
  • I found that two batches of wrapper dough with 300g flour + 150g water was about perfect to use up all of the filling and have a good-performing wrapper (~18 dumplings each).
  • Leftover filling can be consumed with a small batch of wrappers weighed out to the appropriate (very small) amount using the appropriate hydration ratio.
  • Note: % hydration = 100*liquid mass/flour mass
  • for 300g of flour, 150g of water was almost too soft (50% hydration), and ~120 to ~130g was a bit too firm (40-43% hydration)
  • Wetting the edges of the wrappers is totally unnecessary
  • Prepare one small baking pan in top part of kitchen freezer with parchment paper, and a larger pan in the basement freeser. Also prepare space in the basement freezer for a bag to fill with dumplings.
  • Working solo, wrappers and dumplings can be made in series using a small cereal bowl with a damp dish cloth to hold wrapperr dough and small dough balls.
  • Prepare small area to dredge wrappers in flour so they don't stick. Only make about 1/3 of the wrappers in each batch (i.e. 1/6 of total wrappers) at a time
  • Loosen and warm small dough balls in hand by squishing.
  • Press 4 times with palm
  • Roll 4x rotating 90 degrees
  • Roll ~6x toward edges from middle
  • Add filling: maybe ~40% of wrapper diameter?
  • stretch and use three fingers on each side to seal middle and each end.
  • Twist and shear edges to crimp closed.
  • Store on plate as you make them, then transfer to upstairs freezer and then downstairs freezer, and then bag.
  • Dumplings can also be cooked in a steamer basket lined with a hankie for 15-20 minutes.
  • DO NOT MAKE ENORMOUS DUMPLINGS -- they will take longer to steam.
