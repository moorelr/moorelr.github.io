Chicken & Dumplins

Rev. 10/9/2022 LRM.

I've always been intimidated by chicken & dumplins -- turns out to be pretty easy!
Dumpling dough adapted from the Vice Chicken and Dumplings Recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken stock -- ~2 cups
  • Carrots -- 2 whole carrots sliced
  • Cabbage -- quarter of a cabbage, coarseley chopped
  • Onion -- 1 medium onion sliced
  • Garlic -- 1/2 bulb of garlic, minced
  • Chicken -- 1 lb chicken thighs
  • Cooking oil -- 1 tbsp

  • Flour -- 2 cups of all purpose flour
  • Baking powder -- 2 tsp
  • Salt -- 2 tsp
  • Black pepper -- 1 tsp, ground
  • Milk -- 3/4 cup whole milk
  • Butter -- 1 cube (2 tbsp) of butter, melted
  • Egg -- 1 large egg

Instructions

  1. Thaw frozen stock in a separate pot if necessary
  2. Pre-cut vegetables, thaw and debone meat if necessary
  3. Boil meat in chicken stock for 20 minutes, check the temperature, then remove and shred with a fork.
  4. Sautee vegetables in oil for ~10 minutes until fully cooked (lid on), and then pour in broth and shredded chicken and bring to simmer on low heat


  5. Prepare dumpling dough by whisking together dry and wet ingredients separately and then adding wet to dry.
  6. Stir batter/dough just until completely combined
  7. Use a couple of spoons to transfer dumplings into simmmering soup, and allow 15 minutes to cook
  8. Serve, and then add boiling water and more dumplings to the soup as needed to use up all of the dumpling batter
  9. It turns out that the dumplings refrigerate and reheat well. I was worried that they might disintegrate or something, but they were just as good leftover!
